
ANALYTICAL REPORT ON THE VEGETABLE OILS AND FATS MARKET 

KEY EVENTS 14 – 22 JUNE, 2026 

Regional Coverage: European Union (EU-27), United Kingdom, Balkan Region, Central Asia, and the Caucasus. 

 

I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY  

In June 2026, the vegetable oil market is experiencing a pronounced correction due to excess supply from South America and the active export policy of Asian countries. For the Central Asia and Caucasus region, the key trend remains the transition from simple processing to deep integration into global supply chains, which requires strict compliance with traceability standards (EUDR). Despite the decline in global prices, logistics premiums in the Black Sea region remain high, creating a price gap between raw materials and finished products. 

Main trends of the reporting period: 

Price normalization: Global vegetable oil prices were under pressure during the reporting period due to record harvests in South America and policy adjustments in Indonesia. 

Risk premium and harvest: In the Black Sea region, increased logistics costs and risk premiums remain, while harvest expectations in Russia and Ukraine support pressure on prices for vegetable oils and raw materials. 

Regional transformation: Central Asia shows a trend towards deepening processing and the formation of chains with higher added value, but import dependence remains, and the transition to sustainable localization of production is in the process of formation. 

Regulatory barriers: The implementation of EUDR and RED III strengthens requirements for traceability and documentation of sustainability, so certified supply chains, including ISCC, are becoming an important condition for access to a number of regulated and premium markets. 

Strategic change in logistics: the role of regional corridors, including the Trans-Caspian Route (TITR), is increasing as an element of diversification of supplies and reducing logistics risks against the backdrop of geopolitical instability.  

Adjustment of the trade policy of the Russian Federation: on June 10, 2026, the Government of the Russian Federation adopted a resolution to increase import customs duties on palm oil and its fractions from unfriendly countries to 35% from June 11, 2026 instead of the previous 5%; an exception is made for Hungary and Slovakia.

III. REGIONAL MARKETS ANALYSIS  

1. Major Western and Northern European Markets 

The market situation in Western and Northern Europe as of mid-June 2026 is characterized by growth in oilseed production while maintaining high volatility in energy prices.  

Germany 

Germany has stated that trade with Indonesia has not been adversely affected by the introduction of the OneGate system, which simplifies export procedures. Despite concerns that the new system could complicate interactions between the countries, German authorities noted that trade volumes remain stable, and both sides continue to actively cooperate. This indicates that changes in Indonesia's export policy have not had a negative impact on economic relations with Germany. Indonesia reported that its trade with Germany had totaled roughly $6.1 billion in 2025. Throughout last year, German-bound exports under the sub-category “vegetable oils/animal fats” hit $78.6 million in 2025. Shipments dropped almost 70% year-on-year to just $8.2 million from January to April 2026. Palm oil falls under this sub-category. 

Netherlands 

Indonesian Defense Minister Prabowo Subianto held a phone call with Dutch Prime Minister Mark Rutte, during which they discussed trade issues and security in the Hormuz Strait. Both leaders emphasized the importance of cooperation between their countries to ensure stability in the region and enhance economic relations. Prabowo also expressed interest in deepening bilateral engagement, which could contribute to the development of trade and security in East Asia. 

France 

France anticipates a larger winter barley harvest this year, alongside stable rapeseed production levels. The country's agricultural agency has reported that favorable weather conditions and improved farming practices are contributing to the expected increase in barley output. Meanwhile, rapeseed yields are projected to remain consistent, reflecting steady agricultural management. Overall, these developments indicate a positive outlook for French crop production in the upcoming season. 

Belgium 

Cargill has announced a €56 million investment to enhance its food manufacturing and innovation capabilities in Belgium. This investment aims to expand the company's production facilities and improve its ability to develop new food products, thereby strengthening its position in the European market. The initiative is part of Cargill's broader strategy to meet increasing consumer demand for innovative food solutions and to support sustainable practices in food production. 

Market Overview (Western Europe) 

The Balkan railways are undergoing significant modernization aimed at enhancing sustainability, interoperability, and green innovation. This initiative focuses on upgrading infrastructure and adopting advanced technologies to improve efficiency and reduce environmental impact. By investing in electric and hybrid trains, as well as implementing smart railway systems, the region aims to create a more sustainable transportation network that meets the growing demand for eco-friendly travel options. This transformation not only supports regional connectivity but also aligns with broader European goals for sustainable transport. 

2. Southern, Central, and Eastern European Regional Markets  

Poland 

Poland's Orlen Group has officially launched a new biofuels plant with an investment of €189 million, marking a significant step towards enhancing the country's renewable energy capabilities. The facility is designed to produce advanced biofuels, contributing to the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions and supporting Poland's transition to a more sustainable energy landscape. This development aligns with Orlen's commitment to diversifying its energy portfolio and meeting the growing demand for cleaner fuel alternatives in the transportation sector. 

Estonia 

Estonia has inaugurated one of its largest biomethane facilities in Viljandi, which will process waste to produce eco-friendly biomethane. This state-of-the-art plant is designed to generate up to 20,000 megawatt-hours of energy annually, enough to power approximately 3,000 households. The facility supports Estonia's efforts to reduce carbon emissions and increase the share of renewable energy in its energy mix. The project was made possible through collaboration between the public and private sectors, along with funding from European sources. 

4. Production and Balances in the Black Sea Region 

In the Black Sea region the balances of the Black Sea region remain in the revision phase, while the expected increase in sunflower seed production supports the export potential of the region, but logistical constraints and seasonal unevenness of supplies maintain price volatility. 

Ukraine 

The oilseed processing sector continues to be tense: low margins and limited stocks of raw materials remained constraining factors for processors, despite the industry's attempts to focus on the export of high value-added products. The Ukrainian sunflower season may become one of the weakest in over a decade. The main reason remains the low profitability of processing: export prices for sunflower oil do not provide sufficient margin for processors, forcing some plants to reduce loading or switch to rapeseed processing, where the economics currently look more attractive. 

Russia 

Russia is now the world's largest producer of sunflower seed. The harvest is expected to reach a new record high in the coming season due to a significant expansion in crop area and a slight increase in yields. The US Department of Agriculture (USDA) has recently presented its June estimate for global oilseed production. The estimate indicates that Russian sunflower seed output in the upcoming crop year will reach 19.5 million tonnes. This would represent an increase of roughly 11 per cent compared with the current season and would mark a new record crop. 

Starting June 15, Russia will increase the import duty on palm oil from unfriendly countries to 35%. This decision is part of the government's efforts to protect domestic producers and respond to the economic sanctions imposed by these countries. The move aims to reduce reliance on imported palm oil and promote local alternatives in the market.

4. Central Asia and the Caucasus 

The Central Asia and Caucasus region is demonstrating an active transformation phase in the oil and fat industry in June 2026, driven by countries' aspirations for import substitution and the growth of export potential for processed products.  

Kazakhstan 

KazAgroFinance JSC and the Islamic Corporation for the Development of the Private Sector (ICD) have signed a Memorandum of Cooperation, granting the company access to Islamic financing of up to 20 billion tenge to support agricultural producers. The funds will be used to finance the acquisition of agricultural machinery and equipment, as well as to update fixed assets. According to KazAgroFinance, the financing will be provided to clients through the Islamic leasing mechanism of Ijara under attractive, favorable conditions. All terms of the agreement will be predetermined, fixed for the duration of the contract, and fully compliant with the strict ethical standards of Islamic finance. Independent director Rustam Abdusalamov stated that the company intends to continue expanding its support tools for the agricultural sector in Kazakhstan. 

Kazakhstan has confirmed its status as a key producer and exporter of fat-and-oil products in Central Asia: at the end of 2025, the industry's export revenue reached 963 million US dollars, and Uzbekistan, China and Tajikistan remain the main markets. In June 2026, for the first time, a batch of rapeseed oil was sent through the port of Aktau, which confirms the development of the trans-Caspian logistics route and the diversification of export supplies. For Uzbekistan and the countries of the Caucasus, no new confirmed events for June 2026 were detected in the submitted sources. 

Export of rapeseed oil reached a record high due to rising demand from neighboring countries. This surge is attributed to favorable weather conditions that boosted rapeseed production, alongside the country's strategic efforts to enhance its agricultural exports.  

Uzbekistan 

Uzbekistan has sent 320 tons of humanitarian aid to Balkh, Afghanistan, in response to the ongoing humanitarian crisis in the region. This aid consists of essential supplies, including food and medical assistance, aimed at supporting the Afghan population facing severe hardships. The initiative underscores Uzbekistan's commitment to regional stability and cooperation, as well as its role in providing crucial support to neighboring countries in need. 

Tajikistan 

A World Trade Organization (WTO)-backed program has significantly enhanced food safety standards in Tajikistan, leading to notable growth in the country's export capabilities. This initiative focuses on improving agricultural practices and regulatory frameworks, which has resulted in increased confidence among international buyers regarding the quality of Tajik products. As a result, Tajikistan is better positioned to expand its market presence and boost its economy through enhanced trade opportunities. 

Turkmenistan  

Turkmenistan has proposed the export of textile and food products to Iraq as part of efforts to strengthen bilateral trade relations. This initiative aims to diversify Turkmenistan's export markets and enhance economic cooperation between the two countries. By promoting its high-quality textiles and food items, Turkmenistan seeks to tap into the growing demand in Iraq, thereby fostering mutual benefits and expanding its economic footprint in the region. 

Azerbaijan 

Azerbaijan is preparing to implement Islamic banking plans, with the goal of issuing its first sukuk by 2028. This initiative aims to diversify the country's financial system and attract investments in accordance with Islamic finance principles. By developing a framework for Islamic banking, Azerbaijan seeks to enhance its economic growth and provide new financial products that cater to both local and international investors, positioning itself as a competitive player in the regional financial landscape.

IV. LOGISTICS AND SUPPLY SECURITY: COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS 

Kazakhstan strengthens positions in Iran: Kazakhstan will receive a plot of land in the Iranian port of Shahid Rajaee, as announced by the Association of Kazakhstan's Freight Rail Carriers. After completing the necessary procedures, the land will be handed over to Kazakhstan to develop its transport and logistics infrastructure. Iran has agreed to allocate this land on the northern shore of the Hormuz Strait, which will facilitate the construction of terminals and a logistics center for the transshipment of Kazakh and transit cargo from Central Asian countries. This project is expected to strengthen Kazakhstan's position in the southern direction and provide access to international maritime routes. 

The Middle Corridor (TITR): The President of Montenegro, Jakov Milatović, and the Prime Minister of Kazakhstan, Olzhas Bektenov, discussed the connection of the Montenegrin port of Bar with the Trans-Caspian International Transport Route (TITR) in Astana, as reported by TASS. This connection will provide Asian countries with an alternative route to Southern and Central Europe, bypassing Russia. The participants highlighted Montenegro's strategically advantageous location and its contribution to ensuring access to the Adriatic Sea. The integration of TITR's capacities with the port of Bar was also discussed, which would create a new multimodal corridor to Southern and Central Europe. Over the past year, the volume of transportation along the Trans-Caspian route increased by more than 36%, reaching 77,000 TEUs, and overall cargo turnover tripled from 1.5 million to 4.5 million tons compared to 2022, with plans to increase it to 10 million tons in the coming years.  

In Brussels, following the international conference on the development of the Trans-Caspian International Transport Route (TITR), four agreements were signed totaling $462 million. The agreements aim to enhance transport infrastructure and strengthen connectivity between Europe and Asia. 

V. CONCLUSION: IMPLICATIONS FOR THE MALAYSIAN PALM OIL INDUSTRY 

Re-export to Ukraine and Georgia from Turkey: It is necessary to develop alternative routes for the re-export of Malaysian palm oil from Turkey towards Ukraine (the import potential exceeds 200,000 tons per year), Georgia, and Azerbaijan. 

Creating a Strategic Hub in Tashkent: Expedite the consideration of the official proposal from Uzbekistan’s Ambassador Karomidin Gadoev to establish a regional distribution logistics warehouse for Malaysian oils in Tashkent. The warehouse should be operational and stocked before the scheduled review of customs duties in the CIS, planned for 2027. 

"Counter-Labeling" Campaign in Serbia: Turn the mandatory implementation of the "yellow triangle" in the Balkans into a marketing advantage. Launch an targeted promotional campaign among Serbian B2B clients, positioning certified palm oil (MSPO) not as a cheap substitute, but as an eco-friendly, stable, heart-healthy, and drought-protected alternative to European soft oils facing severe climate pressure. 

Filling the Uzbek Industrial Vacuum: Leverage the fact that 63% of processing capacities in Uzbekistan are currently idle. Offer direct contracts for the supply of Malaysian palm oil as a primary feedstock for local margarine production, effectively bridging their gap. 

Shifting Premium Fats to the Middle Corridor (TITR): Diversify supply chains for specialty fats bound for the confectionery industry. Redirect them from the ocean route (where spot freight has soared to $10,000) to the land-based Middle Corridor with its guaranteed 15-day transit and stable insurance environment.
