Extended Weekly Update 20.7.2026

ANALYTICAL REPORT ON THE VEGETABLE OILS AND FATS MARKET
KEY EVENTS 13 – 20 JULY, 2026
Regional Coverage: European Union (EU-27), United Kingdom, Balkan Region, Central Asia, and the Caucasus.

I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

  • Drop in EU Palm Oil Imports: Total purchases fell by over 5% to 2.85 million tonnes due to the exclusion of palm-based biofuels from tax incentives in Germany and France.

  • Record Rapeseed Crushing: The EU hit a historical crushing record of 26.3 million tonnes, driven by high capacity in the Netherlands, Germany, and France.

  • German Logistics Crisis: A summer heatwave caused critical water drops in the Rhine River, lowering barge capacity, spiking tariffs, and risking feedstock supply chains.

  • Explosive HVO Demand: German demand for Hydrotreated Vegetable Oil is projected to jump to 2.1 million tonnes in 2026 (up from 800,000 tonnes in 2025) due to RED III implementation.

  • Restricting direct imports from Ukraine forced Polish crushers to actively compete with Western European plants for Romania's 1.5 million tonne rapeseed export potential.

  • Attacks on Ukrainian Infrastructure: Drone strikes on July 11–14 completely destroyed 34,000 tonnes of sunflower oil and 45,000 tonnes of wheat at Kernel's Odesa terminal, freezing operations indefinitely.

  • The USDA cut Ukraine’s 2026/27 sunflower harvest forecast to 14.5 million tonnes, balancing the global market against an increase in Russia’s forecast to 18 million tonnes.

  • Surge in Russian Exports: Russian vegetable oil shipments since the beginning of the year grew by 18% (exceeding 1 million tonnes), despite a nearly threefold export duty hike on July 1st.

  • Export Dynamics: Sunflower oil shipments surged by 46% (to 191,300 tonnes), rapeseed oil grew by 15%, and linseed oil jumped 9-fold (to 26,300 tonnes). The main buyers in July are China (+18%), Turkey (a 5-fold increase), and Libya (a 6-fold increase). Final July shipments face headwinds from rising freight tariffs, escalating vessel insurance costs, and overall complications in the Black Sea-Azov basin.

III. REGIONAL MARKETS ANALYSIS

1. Major Western and Northern European Markets

In the 2025/26 period, European Union (EU) member states saw a decline in palm oil imports, totaling approximately 2.85 million tonnes, which represents a decrease of over 5% compared to the previous year. This reduction is attributed to changes in legal requirements across member states, particularly in Germany and France, where palm oil-based biofuels were excluded from national quota obligations and tax incentives due to their classification related to indirect land use change (iLUC).

Despite the overall decline, the Netherlands remained the leading importer of palm oil within the EU, with imports rising to about 1.05 million tonnes, a 9% increase from the previous year. Italy, however, experienced a 12% drop in imports to nearly 734,000 tonnes. Conversely, Spain and Germany increased their imports by 35% and 20%, respectively, driven by growing demand from the food and chemical industries. Meanwhile, Denmark and Sweden saw significant decreases in palm oil imports, dropping to 52,000 tonnes and 38,000 tonnes, respectively.

UK

New crop rapeseed prices for Erith for November contracts were polled at £471.50/t (+£14.00 per week). Deliveries for February 2027 strengthened to £475.00/t. Rapeseed purchase prices for delivery to Liverpool and East Anglia for November showed an increase of £15.50 for the reporting week, reaching £473.00/t and £470.50/t, respectively.

France

During the week from July 10 to 17, 2026, November rapeseed futures on the Paris Euronext exchange (MATIF) showed a 4% increase (+€22.00/t) and closed at €550.00/t. The futures contract successfully broke through the key technical resistance level of €541–545/t, moving towards the next calculated resistance level of €560/t. The Relative Strength Index (RSI) rose from 50 to 66 points over the week, approaching the overbought zone.

On July 17, 2026, the French Ministry of Agriculture published the official crop production forecast for 2026. Rapeseed production (the primary raw material for rapeseed oil in the country) is projected at 4.63 million tons. This figure remains unchanged compared to the previous year, which analysts view as a neutral factor for the domestic supply and demand balance, ensuring stable utilization of local oilseed crushing and meal production facilities.

Netherlands

A long-term downward trend in crude palm oil (CPO) import volumes continues. At the same time, CPO prices on a CIF Rotterdam basis remain elevated heading into the second half of 2026.The market is supported by limited export availability from key Asian producing nations.

According to the Oil World report published on July 13–14, 2026, the Netherlands was among the key EU countries (alongside Germany and France) that ensured the achievement of a historical record for rapeseed crushing in the European Union — 26.3 million tons. The main driver of processing capacities in Dutch ports is the consistently high demand for rapeseed oil from the local biodiesel industry. At the same time, analysts note that at the start of the 2026/27 season, the Dutch physical market is supplied with raw materials due to high carryover stocks.

Italy

According to the final analytical report on the European biofuels and technical fats market from the UFOP association, released on July 21, 2026, based on the results of the trading season, Italy has firmly established its status as the second-largest importer of palm oil in the European Union (second only to the Netherlands) with a total import volume of 734,000 tons. Despite an overall 12% decrease in imports due to regulatory pressure and the reduction of the biodiesel track, the Italian food and chemical industries in July 2026 are generating consistently high commercial demand for this type of raw material.

In mid-July, the Italian Oil Industry Association (ASSITOL, a member of Federalimentare and Confindustria) issued an official address to the market, condemning the practice of dumping and a "race to the bottom" in lowering olive oil prices by large retail chains (GDO) and the food service sector (Horeca). Anna Cane, President of ASSITOL's olive oil group, emphasized that against the backdrop of a gradual recovery in Mediterranean production volumes following the crisis-level poor harvests of recent years, the industry is obligated to maintain a fair value for Extra Virgin, preventing the devaluation of the labor of farmers and processors.

Germany

During the week of July 13–20, 2026, analysts recorded a sharp jump in projected demand for hydrotreated vegetable oil (HVO) in Germany to 2.1 million tonnes in 2026 (compared to 800,000 tonnes in 2025). The explosive growth is driven by the entry into force of the updated German law implementing the RED III directive, which completely abolished the practice of "double counting" for feedstocks from Annex IX (Part A). This forces German oil companies to physically purchase significantly more vegetable oils and technical fats to meet national greenhouse gas emission reduction quotas.

As of mid-July 2026, physical deliveries of vegetable oils and HVO biodiesel feedstock to German refineries were at risk of disruption due to a critical drop in water levels in the Rhine River amid a summer heatwave. According to trader estimates as of July 14, this significantly reduced barge cargo capacity, driving up domestic logistics tariffs and restricting oil import deliveries from Dutch and Belgian ports to key plants in Western and Southern Germany.

2. Southern, Central, and Eastern European Regional Markets

Romania

Due to severe heat during the harvest period, the European Commission lowered the average rapeseed yield forecast for Romania to 3.08 t/ha (compared to 3.18 t/ha in the previous period). During the reporting week, Romania's domestic market was under simultaneous pressure from the ongoing harvest campaign and high oil price volatility; however, quotes were supported by stable demand from European biodiesel plants.

Against the backdrop of escalating attacks on the port infrastructure of the Black Sea region in mid-July (including a strike on the Kernel terminal in the Odesa region, which destroyed 9,000 tons of sunflower oil), the Romanian port of Constanta has established itself as a key predictable export hub for the region. During the period of July 13–20, truck queues were recorded at the entrance to the port terminals (specifically Dana 32), overwhelmed by transit and local exports of vegetable oils and grain.

Moldova

Moldova sharply increased its sunflower oil exports, shipping 92,000 tons of products to foreign markets for a total of 2.07 billion lei, with an average invoice value of 22.6 lei/kg. This figure is nearly 5 times higher than the weak result of the 2024/25 season (19,210 tons for 437.7 million lei). Local analysts noted Moldova's partial return to the 'major league' of Black Sea vegetable oil exporters, driven by the recovery of domestic seed processing volumes.

Poland

According to market analytical reviews dated July 20, Polish oilseed crushing plants have faced a severe structural shortage of raw materials, as total processing capacities in Poland exceed 4 million tons, while domestic harvest is projected at a level significantly lower than the usual 3.5–3.9 million tons. Due to ongoing restrictions on direct oilseed imports from Ukraine, Polish traders and processors during the reporting week began actively competing with Western European plants for rapeseed import supplies from Romania (whose export potential is estimated at 1.5 million tons) to ensure the planned utilization of their oil crushing capacities in the second half of the year.

3. Production and Balances in the Black Sea Region

Ukraine

In the morning hours of July 14, 2026, a major oilseed transshipment complex belonging to Kernel was severely damaged as a result of a targeted drone attack in the Odesa region. The resulting large-scale fire destroyed half of the tank farm and terminal infrastructure. In total, approximately 25,000 tons of sunflower oil were damaged and destroyed (including volumes belonging to Kernel and an American company leasing the capacity). This occurred immediately following the attacks on July 11–12, when Kernel had already lost 9,000 tons of oil and 45,000 tons of wheat. The restoration timeline for the terminal has been frozen pending a full engineering audit.

Raw material losses and the partial suspension of deep-water shipments have sharply reduced the available market supply. Demand prices for crude sunflower oil delivered to Black Sea ports rose by $10–20 over the week, reaching $1345–1350/t. Prices on CIF Rotterdam terms in the European physical market remained at peak levels of $1490/t amid the Black Sea shortage. Export quotations for sunflower oil on an FOB Black Sea basis stopped falling and stabilized at $1380/t.

In the July report of the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA), published in the middle of the month, the forecast for the sunflower harvest in Ukraine for the 2026/27 season was reduced from 15 million tons to 14.5 million tons. This downward adjustment partially balanced the global market, offsetting a simultaneous increase in the sunflower crop forecast for Russia (from 17.5 to 18.0 million tons).

According to Spike Brokers as of July 13, 2026, the Ukrainian market approached the end of the season with historically low sunflower seed carryover stocks. Intense competition among crushing plants for the last available lots is keeping domestic purchase prices at a consistently high level — averaging $721/t CPT plant.

According to statistics published on July 15 by the Solvent Extractors Association of India (SEA), India sharply increased its sunflower oil imports to 240,000 tons in June–July (compared to 190,000 tons in May). Ukraine remains one of the top suppliers to the Indian market. The growth in demand was driven by the narrowing of the price discount of palm oil relative to soybean and sunflower oils to below the $50/t level, making Ukrainian sunflower oil commercially more attractive to foreign buyers.

Russia

As of July 10–16, the total volume of Russian vegetable oil shipments to foreign markets since the beginning of 2026 has exceeded 1 million tons, which is 18% higher than the figures for the same period in 2025 (863 thousand tons). By crop, the following dynamics were recorded: rapeseed oil exports increased by 15% (to 724.4 thousand tons), sunflower oil by 46% (to 191.3 thousand tons), and linseed oil surged 9-fold (to 26.3 thousand tons). Key sales destinations in July are China (+18%), Turkey (a 5-fold increase), and Libya (a 6-fold increase).

According to the price monitoring of the analytical agency OleoScope, on July 15, 2026, the export cost of sunflower oil on a FOB Black Sea basis stopped the prolonged decline of previous weeks and stabilized at $1,380/t. Meanwhile, on a Rotterdam basis (FOB bulk), the cost of offers for the supply of sunflower oil on July 14–17 maintained its highest values in the last 3 months at $1,490/t, due to a general shortage of physical raw material offers on the global market.

During the reporting week, import demand for Russian sunflower oil from India and China remained steadily high, which offset the pressure from the export duty that increased on July 1st (raised by the Ministry of Agriculture nearly threefold to 3,294.2 rubles/t). However, as industry analysts note, the pace of physical shipments in the second half of July is being constrained by logistical complications, rising freight costs, and vessel insurance in the Black Sea-Azov basin.

4. Central Asia and the Caucasus

Kazakhstan

On July 14–18, 2026, official statistical indicators for the processing industry for the completed reporting period (January–April 2026) were released, recording sharp growth in the oil and fat sector. Production of refined and unrefined sunflower oil in Kazakhstan reached 335.5 thousand tons (+13.2% compared to the same period last year). At the same time, the volume of sunflower oil exports soared by almost 50%, reaching 353.8 thousand tons. The main external sales markets for Kazakh oil plants during the reporting week are Uzbekistan, China, Afghanistan, Tajikistan, and Iran. The share of imported sunflower oil in the domestic market of the Republic of Kazakhstan fell to a historic low of 12.5%.

During the meteorological meetings of the Ministry of Agriculture of the Republic of Kazakhstan regarding preparations for the harvest campaign, it was announced that, as part of diversification efforts, the country has reached a historic record for oilseed crop areas — 4.3 million hectares. The share of oilseeds in Kazakhstan's total crop structure rose to 18%, while the area for less profitable wheat decreased by 125,000 hectares.

On July 20, 2026, Prime Minister of Kazakhstan Olzhas Bektenov signed a government decree approving a new three-year plan to combat the shadow economy. In addition to the total digitalization of the agro-industrial complex (satellite monitoring of oilseed land use and automation of subsidies), the document introduces strict changes to the regulation of retail and wholesale trade: starting from 2027, mandatory digital labeling of vegetable oil will be introduced in Kazakhstan. This measure is aimed at whitening the market for liquid oils and fats, as well as suppressing gray imports and counterfeiting in the sector.

Uzbekistan

In mid-July 2026, official trade balances were released, according to which Uzbekistan recorded a sharp jump in vegetable oil imports from the Republic of Kazakhstan — the volume of supplies for the nine months of the current marketing year increased by 42%, reaching a record high of 312 thousand tons. During the reporting week, Kazakh sunflower oil holds dominant positions both in the industrial processing sector (margarines, confectionery fats) and in the republic's retail market.

During trading at the Uzbek Republican Commodity Exchange (UZEX) during the 29th week (July 13–19, 2026), weighted average prices for imported crude sunflower oil and local refined cottonseed oil entered a stabilization phase. Seasonal price pressure from record import volumes from the CIS was offset by macroeconomic risks: in mid-July, the Central Bank of Uzbekistan confirmed an increase in inflationary pressure due to rising global logistics costs and alternative transport routes, which is preventing domestic wholesale fat prices from falling.

Georgia

According to updated statistical data and estimates for the July reporting period, Georgia's expenditures on sunflower oil purchases show steady growth against the backdrop of increased export duties by the key supplier (Russia). Geostat statistics record a significant value discrepancy: while the physical volume of imports grew only slightly (+3.6%), Georgia's monetary costs for oil procurement jumped by 18.6%. The average cost per imported ton of sunflower oil at the Georgian border rose from $1,370 to $1,568/t.

According to Geostat analytical data, Russia maintains its status as the absolute leader, accounting for 80% of all sunflower oil imports to Georgia. The volume of Russian vegetable oil imports in value terms nearly doubled, reaching $5.2 million. In the import structure recorded by specialized agencies in mid-July 2026, Ukraine holds the second position in terms of shipment volumes to the Georgian market, with a figure of $607.6 thousand.

III REGULATORY OUTLOOK

EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR)

On July 13, 2026, the European Commission officially adopted a Delegated Act amending the scope of the Deforestation Regulation (update of Annex I) and Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/1565. Complex livestock processing products, such as finished cattle leather, raw hides and skins of cattle, car seats, retreaded tires, as well as soybeans for sowing, were completely excluded from the compliance requirements. At the same time, the European Commission officially included palm oil derivatives used for the production of oleochemicals, certain types of soap, and instant coffee in the regulated list.

Strict exemption criteria have been introduced for certain types of palm oil-based oleochemical compounds. Derivatives used exclusively as raw materials for the production of medicinal and veterinary products under EU pharmaceutical legislation are not subject to the EUDR.

The EU's Renewable Energy Directive III (RED III)

Although the RED III Directive had entered into force earlier, in the first half of July 2026, the European biofuel and oilseed market was influenced by a crucial decision of the European Parliament dated July 8–9, 2026. The Parliament officially rejected Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/2680, which was intended to tighten RED II/III standards and classify soybean oil as a high indirect land-use change (high ILUC) risk feedstock. Had the act been adopted, the use of soy-based biodiesel in the EU would have been subject to a progressive reduction leading to a total ban by 2030. The position of FEDIOL and the European crushers' coalition, advocated in July, allowed for the temporary protection of the investment climate and the profitability of soybean crushing in the EU.

On July 22, 2026, the Spanish Council of Ministers approved the national regulatory framework to meet transport decarbonization targets in accordance with RED III. Simultaneously, the German market maintained price momentum for hydrotreated vegetable oil (HVO) in mid-July, driven by a previous decision of the Bundestag to abolish double counting for oils from Annex IX (Part A), which kept spot quotes for biofuels and technical fats at peak levels in July 2026.

IV. LOGISTICS AND SUPPLY SECURITY: COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS

Uzbekistan records a 42% increase in the transit and import of vegetable oils from Kazakhstan, reaching 312,000 tons, which requires the expansion of logistics infrastructure. Amid delays in the transportation of liquid cargo, Middle Corridor countries are beginning to synchronize digital documentation to eliminate barriers. Meanwhile, Tajikistan is considering leasing Russian farmland due to rising rail freight costs, while Kazakhstan is attracting €2 billion in EU investment to modernize the ports of Aktau and Kuryk.

V. CONCLUSION: IMPLICATIONS FOR THE MALAYSIAN PALM OIL INDUSTRY

Re-export to Ukraine and Georgia from Turkey: It is necessary to develop alternative routes for the re-export of Malaysian palm oil from Turkey towards Ukraine, Georgia, and Azerbaijan.

Creating a Strategic Hub in Tashkent: Expedite the consideration of the official proposal from Uzbekistan’s Ambassador Karomidin Gadoev to establish a regional distribution logistics warehouse for Malaysian oils in Tashkent.

Shifting Premium Fats to the Middle Corridor (TITR): Diversify supply chains for specialty fats bound for the confectionery industry. Redirect them from the ocean route (where spot freight has soared to $10,000) to the land-based Middle Corridor with its guaranteed 15-day transit and stable insurance environment.
